Objective

To foster critical thinking, persuasive communication, and teamwork among AIChE student members through structured debates on engineering and sustainability topics.

1. Structure & Participation

  • Eligibility: Active AIChE student chapters in the Southern Asia region.
  • Team Composition: Each participating chapter nominates two representatives: one pre-final year student and one final year student.
  • Grouping: Random assignment to Team Government or Team Opposition. If uneven, extra chapter(s) placed in one team; scores normalized for fairness.
  • Communication: All selected participants will be added to a dedicated WhatsApp group for updates and the topic will be disclosed 48 hours before the event.

2. Debate Format

  • Style: Parliamentary format.
  • Speaking Time: Each chapter gets 5 minutes total — Speaker 1 (Junior): 2 minutes; Speaker 2 (Senior): 3 minutes. Exceeding time → point deductions.
  • Rebuttal Round: Optional (1 minute per team) if time allows.

3. Judging Criteria (Total 100 points)

Criteria Description Points
Content & Logic Strength, structure, and depth of arguments 30
Team Coordination Flow and balance between the two speakers 25
Delivery & Confidence Clarity, tone, and stage presence 25
Relevance & Impact Connection to engineering or sustainability issues 20

4. Scoring & Recognition

  • Evaluation: Judges from the AIChE Executive Committee.
  • Awards: Winning Team (total points) and Best Speaker (individual).
  • Recognition: Winners featured on AIChE Executive Committee social media platforms.

5. Participation Certificates

All participants will receive digital certificates from AIChE Southern Asia recognising their contribution and public speaking effort.

6. Impact & Chapter Activity Points

Participating chapters will earn AIChE Activity Points contributing to annual report evaluation and strengthening active chapter status.
